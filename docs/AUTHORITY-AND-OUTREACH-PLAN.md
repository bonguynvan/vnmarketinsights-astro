# Off-Page Authority & Outreach Plan

**Status:** Proposed
**Last updated:** 2026-06-22
**Goal:** Move the "Vietnam X market" cluster from page 5–10 to page 1–2.

---

## Why this, why now

On-page is largely done: the report pages exist, carry **fresh 2025 data with sources**,
have schema, internal links, and a clean editorial design. GSC shows strong **impressions**
on the money queries (`vietnam payments market` 36, `mobile payments market` 31, `digital
payment market` 25, `market overview` 25…) but **position 56–84** — i.e. Google knows the
pages are relevant but doesn't trust the domain enough to rank them. That gap is **authority
(links + brand signals)**, not content. So the lever now is off-page + E-E-A-T.

Our unfair advantage vs. the paywalled vendors (Statista, Mordor, IMARC): we are **free,
fresh, specific, and citable**. Free data pages earn links that paywalled PDFs cannot.

---

## Track 1 — Make the reports *linkable assets* (on-site, do first)

Authority compounds only if there's something worth linking to. Sharpen the reports into
"citable data" pages:

- [ ] **"Cite this report" block** on each report (pre-formatted citation + canonical URL +
      "last updated"). Journalists/bloggers copy-paste it → natural attribution links.
- [ ] **One original data view per report** that *only we* publish (the charts already help).
      Strongest candidate: a **time-series from the pipeline** (e.g. topic momentum, QR
      growth) that updates monthly — vendors can't match the freshness.
- [ ] **A flagship "State of Vietnam Digital Economy 2026"** single page that aggregates the
      best stats across sectors — the natural thing to link when someone needs "one source."
- [ ] **E-E-A-T signals**: a named author/editor with a short bio + `sameAs`, an explicit
      methodology/sources page (exists at `/sources` — strengthen it), and visible
      "reviewed on" dates (done via the freshness line).

## Track 2 — Digital PR / data journalism (highest-ROI links)

Data sites win links by being *quoted*. Turn pipeline output into pitchable stories:

- [ ] **Monthly data drop**: "Vietnam QR payments grew +160% YoY" type findings → a short
      press-style post + email to reporters who cover Vietnam (VnExpress International,
      Vietnam Briefing, Tech in Asia, e27, The Investor, Nikkei Asia VN desk).
- [ ] **Reactive PR (newsjacking)**: when GSO/SBV release quarterly data, publish a clean
      visual summary *same day* and notify journalists — fast, free, linkable.
- [ ] **HARO / Qwoted / journalist requests**: answer "Vietnam market/fintech/e-commerce"
      queries with our data + a link.

## Track 3 — Targeted link building

- [ ] **Unlinked brand mentions**: monitor for "vnmarketinsights" / our stats being cited
      without a link → request the link.
- [ ] **Resource-page links**: "Vietnam market research / data resources" lists (university
      Asia-business guides, export-promotion agencies, chamber-of-commerce sites,
      market-entry consultancies). Pitch the free reports.
- [ ] **Guest data contributions**: offer a Vietnam data snapshot to ASEAN/SEA business blogs
      and newsletters in exchange for attribution.
- [ ] **Competitor backlink mining**: pull backlinks of Vietnam Briefing / Statista's free VN
      pages (Ahrefs/Semrush free tier or similar) → pitch the same sites our free equivalent.
- [ ] **Wikipedia / Wikidata**: where a relevant article cites a stat, our sourced page can be
      a citation (only where genuinely the best free source — no spam).

## Track 4 — Distribution / brand signals (indirect authority)

- [ ] **Weekly brief → real newsletter** (infra exists: lead capture + `LEAD_WEBHOOK_URL`).
      Subscribers = repeat visits = brand/engagement signals.
- [ ] **LinkedIn / X**: post each chart as an image with the data + link. The
      affiliate/seller and market-data audiences are active there.
- [ ] **Republish briefs** as LinkedIn articles / Medium with canonical back to the site.
- [ ] **Answer communities**: Reddit (r/VietNam, r/ecommerce), Quora, seller forums — link
      the relevant guide/report where genuinely helpful.

---

## Cadence (sustainable, solo-friendly)

| Weekly | Monthly | Quarterly |
|---|---|---|
| 1 social post per chart; answer 1–2 journalist/community queries; check unlinked mentions | 1 data-drop story + journalist email; 1 resource-page outreach batch (10 targets) | 1 flagship asset refresh ("State of…"); review GSC position movement on the money cluster |

## What to measure

- **Referring domains** (the lead indicator — links precede ranking).
- **Average position** on the money cluster (target: 56–84 → <20 in ~2 quarters).
- **Impressions → clicks**: once position breaks into page 1–2, CTR should finally convert
  the existing impressions.
- **Branded search** (`vnmarketinsights`, `vietnam search insights`) as a brand-signal proxy.

## Honest expectations

Authority is slow: meaningful movement on a competitive cluster is **1–3 months per step**,
and the report-vendor SERPs are entrenched. The realistic early wins are the **lower-comp
long-tail** (sellers, comparisons, specific stats) and **branded/navigational** queries; the
big "vietnam payments market" head terms come after referring domains accumulate. Prioritize
Track 1 + Track 2 — one genuinely-cited data study beats fifty low-quality links.
