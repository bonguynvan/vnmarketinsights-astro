# Regulatory Environment for Digital Business Vietnam

## Executive Summary

Vietnam's regulatory framework for digital businesses has evolved rapidly, balancing encouragement of digital innovation with consumer protection, data security, and national sovereignty concerns. The legal landscape encompasses e-commerce, fintech, data protection, cybersecurity, and foreign investment regulations.

## Regulatory Architecture

### Primary Regulators

#### Ministry of Industry and Trade (MOIT)
- **Scope**: E-commerce, digital trade
- **Key department**: Vietnam E-commerce and Digital Economy Agency
- **Responsibilities**: Licensing, consumer protection, market oversight

#### State Bank of Vietnam (SBV)
- **Scope**: Payment services, fintech, digital banking
- **Responsibilities**: Licensing, monetary policy, financial stability
- **Enforcement**: Bank inspections, penalties

#### Ministry of Information and Communications (MIC)
- **Scope**: Telecommunications, internet, content
- **Key agency**: Authority of Information Security
- **Responsibilities**: Licensing, content regulation, cybersecurity

#### Ministry of Public Security
- **Scope**: Cybersecurity, data protection enforcement
- **Key agency**: Cybersecurity Department
- **Responsibilities**: National security, cybercrime

#### National Digital Transformation Committee
- **Scope**: Coordination across ministries
- **Responsibilities**: Policy coherence, strategy

## Business Establishment

### Legal Entity Options

#### Limited Liability Company (LLC)
- **Foreign ownership**: Up to 100% in most digital services
- **Minimum capital**: No statutory minimum (practical: $10,000+)
- **Timeline**: 30-45 days establishment
- **Governance**: Flexible structure

#### Joint Stock Company
- **Use case**: Larger operations, future IPO
- **Minimum shareholders**: 3
- **Capital**: Divided into shares
- **Reporting**: More stringent requirements

#### Representative Office
- **Limitations**: No commercial activity
- **Use case**: Market research, liaison
- **Timeline**: 15-20 days

### Licensing Requirements

#### E-commerce Service License
- **Applicability**: Marketplace platforms, online retail
- **Requirement**: Decree 52/2013/ND-CP
- **Process**: MOIT application
- **Timeline**: 20-30 days
- **Conditions**: Business registration, website/app disclosure

#### Payment Service License
- **Applicability**: E-wallets, payment gateways
- **Authority**: State Bank of Vietnam
- **Capital**: 50 billion VND (~$2M) minimum
- **Conditions**: Technical infrastructure, security, compliance
- **Timeline**: 90-120 days

#### Financial Services Licenses
- **Lending**: Circular 39/2016/TT-NHNN
- **Insurance distribution**: Ministry of Finance
- **Securities**: State Securities Commission
- **Capital**: Varies by service type

#### Telecommunications/Content Licenses
- **ICP License**: Internet content provision
- **Applicable**: Websites, apps with content
- **Authority**: Ministry of Information and Communications
- **Conditions**: Local server requirements (evolving)

## Foreign Investment Regulations

### Ownership Restrictions

#### Prohibited/Sensitive Sectors
- **Media/content**: Foreign ownership limited
- **Telecommunications**: Strategic partner requirements
- **Payment services**: Ownership caps (49% in some)
- **Land ownership**: Not permitted (lease only)

#### Conditional Sectors
- **E-commerce**: Generally open (100%)
- **Fintech**: Case-by-case approval
- **Education technology**: Partnership models
- **Health tech**: Clinical data restrictions

### Investment Registration
- **Approval process**: Provincial Department of Planning and Investment
- **Documentation**: Business plan, financials, legal docs
- **Timeline**: 15-30 days (straightforward cases)
- **Conditions**: Technology transfer, employment commitments

### Special Economic Zones
- **Software parks**: Preferential treatment (Quang Trung, Saigon Hi-Tech)
- **Benefits**: Tax, land, administrative simplification
- **Requirements**: Technology focus, export orientation

## E-commerce Regulations

### Platform Obligations (Decree 52/2013/ND-CP)

#### Registration and Disclosure
- **Business registration**: Required for all platforms
- **Website disclosure**: Terms, conditions, policies
- **Seller verification**: Identity verification systems
- **Reporting**: Periodic to Ministry of Industry and Trade

#### Consumer Protection
- **Information disclosure**: Product details, pricing
- **7-day return policy**: Standard requirement
- **Complaint handling**: Mechanism required
- **Dispute resolution**: Mediation/arbitration options

#### Prohibited Products
- **List**: Counterfeit, dangerous, illegal goods
- **Enforcement**: Platform liability increasing
- **Monitoring**: Technology-based detection required

### Cross-border E-commerce
- **Import regulations**: Customs, tax compliance
- **Registration**: Foreign platforms (new requirements)
- **Tax**: VAT (10%), import duties above threshold
- **Representative**: Local presence requirements evolving

## Data Protection and Privacy

### Personal Data Protection Decree (2023)

#### Scope and Definitions
- **Personal data**: Any information identifying individual
- **Sensitive data**: Health, biometrics, financial, location
- **Processing**: Collection, storage, use, transfer

#### Data Subject Rights
- **Access**: Right to know data held
- **Correction**: Right to rectify errors
- **Deletion**: Right to erasure (conditions apply)
- **Consent**: Required for processing (with exceptions)

#### Controller Obligations
- **Purpose limitation**: Specific, legitimate purposes
- **Data minimization**: Only necessary data
- **Security**: Appropriate technical measures
- **Breach notification**: 72 hours to authorities

#### Cross-border Transfer
- **Conditions**: Consent, contract, adequacy decisions
- **Localization**: Not blanket requirement
- **Government access**: National security provisions

### Cybersecurity Law (2018)

#### Key Provisions
- **Data localization**: Selective (critical sectors)
- **Content regulation**: Prohibited content categories
- **Emergency powers**: Government intervention rights
- **Provider obligations**: Cooperation, monitoring

#### Implementation
- **Decrees**: Detailed regulations (No. 13, 85)
- **Enforcement**: Ministry of Public Security
- **Penalties**: Fines, suspension, criminal liability

## Fintech Regulations

### Payment Services (Circular 19/2016/TT-NHNN)

#### Licensed Activities
- **Payment accounts**: E-money issuance
- **Payment instruments**: Cards, wallets
- **Payment processing**: Gateway services
- **Aggregation**: Consolidated services

#### Capital Requirements
- **E-wallet**: 50 billion VND minimum
- **Payment gateway**: 20 billion VND
- **Other intermediaries**: 10 billion VND

#### Operational Requirements
- **Segregated accounts**: Customer funds protection
- **AML/CFT**: Anti-money laundering compliance
- **Technology**: Security, availability standards
- **Reporting**: Regular to State Bank of Vietnam

### Digital Banking
- **Neobanks**: Same licensing as traditional
- **Digital-only**: Permitted with physical HQ
- **Foreign**: Joint venture or representative
- **Sandbox**: Innovation testing framework

### Lending and Credit
- **Consumer credit**: Circular 18/2019/TT-NHNN
- **P2P lending**: Regulatory framework pending
- **Crowdfunding**: Limited legal basis
- **BNPL**: Under consumer finance regulations

## Taxation

### Corporate Tax
- **Standard rate**: 20%
- **Incentives**: 10% for tech/software (4 years)
- **Withholding**: Dividends (0%), interest (5%)
- **Transfer pricing**: Documentation requirements

### Value Added Tax (VAT)
- **Standard rate**: 10%
- **Digital services**: B2B and B2C compliance
- **E-commerce**: Platform collection obligations
- **Cross-border**: Reverse charge mechanism

### Digital Services Tax
- **Current**: No separate DST
- **Discussions**: Under consideration
- **International**: Following OECD developments

### E-commerce Tax Compliance
- **Seller obligations**: Income declaration
- **Platform obligations**: Information reporting
- **Payment processors**: Transaction reporting
- **Enforcement**: Increasing coordination

## Employment and Labor

### Digital Economy Employment
- **Contracts**: Written required for 1+ month
- **Benefits**: Social, health, unemployment insurance
- **Working hours**: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week standard
- **Overtime**: Limited, premium rates

### Freelance/Gig Workers
- **Status**: Often informal
- **Protections**: Limited vs employees
- **Trend**: Regulatory attention increasing
- **Platforms**: Joint liability discussions

### Foreign Workers
- **Work permits**: Required for most positions
- **Exceptions**: Intra-company transfers, investors
- **Local hiring**: Preference for certain roles
- **Documentation**: Complex, time-consuming

## Intellectual Property

### Protection Framework
- **Copyright**: Automatic, registration recommended
- **Trademarks**: Registration with NOIP
- **Patents**: Examination process required
- **Trade secrets**: Contractual protection

### Digital Specifics
- **Software**: Copyright protection
- **Algorithms**: Patent possible (criteria strict)
- **Data**: Database rights limited
- **Content**: Platform liability evolving

### Enforcement
- **Administrative**: Market management authorities
- **Civil**: Court proceedings
- **Criminal**: Serious infringement
- **Online**: Takedown procedures

## Compliance and Enforcement

### Monitoring and Inspection
- **Regular audits**: Industry-specific schedules
- **Self-reporting**: Periodic compliance submissions
- **Technology**: Regulator monitoring capabilities
- **Whistleblowing**: Increasingly encouraged

### Penalties and Sanctions
- **Administrative fines**: Increasingly substantial
- **Business suspension**: Temporary or permanent
- **Criminal liability**: For serious violations
- **Reputational**: Public naming and shaming

### Recent Enforcement Trends
